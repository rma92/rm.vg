  • Earth Systems Concepts
    • What is a system?
      • A system is any ordered, interrelated set of things, and their attributes, linked by flows of energy and matter.
      • Inputs: Matter and energy.
      • Outputs: Matter and energy.
    • Open System -- can receive energy and release energy.
    • Closed System -- Self-contained. Does not take in or release energy. Uncommon in nature.
    • Earth is an open system in terms of energy.
      • Sun is outside.
      • Earth gives off energy.
    • Earth system is closed in terms of physical matter and resources. (for the most part. Asteroids and such are negligible.
    • The Forest System
      • Inputs: Sunlight, CO2, O2, Nutrients for roots, Water for roots.
      • Action: Carbs for plant growth from photosynthesis.
      • Output: O2, Water vapor, etc.
    • System Feedback
      • Feedback Loop: Outputs influence system's own operation.
      • Negative Feedback: Discourages change in system; self-regulating, stable condition. e.g. a forest. If you inject predators, the amount of prey will decrease, and the predators will decrease, then stability will return.
      • Positive Feedback: encourages change; runaway/snowballing.
      • Arctic Sea Ice: Albedo positive, or negative feedback.
        • Sun comes in, temperature of ocean increases.
        • Ice melts.
        • Ocean temperature increases some more.
        • More ice melts, etc.
      • Volcanic Eruption.
        1. Volcanic Eruption.
        2. Less sunlight reaching earth surface.
        3. Lower Temperature.
        4. More Snow.
        5. Higher Albedo
        6. Go to 3 (Lower Temperature)
