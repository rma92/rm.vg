Preliminary Work

Fig. 1.1 shows a voltage divider circuit. For this circuit find:
  1. The current I in terms of Vs, R1, and RL; I = Vs / (RL + R1)
  2. The voltage across RL; V = I · RL = (Vs / (RL + R1) ) · RL
  3. The DC power delivered to RL; P = I2 · RL = (Vs / (RL + R1) )2 · RL
  4. The power supplied by the source. P = I2 · (RL + R1) = (Vs / (RL + R1) )2 · (RL + R1)

Discussion

a) Comment on the benefits of using either the DVM or the SCOPE for particular situations.

The DVM is easier to use, as it's ground can be placed anywhere. It cannot, however, measure frequency, the shape of the waveform, or peak-to-peak voltage. In that case, the oscilloscope must be used.

b) Find the current in the circuit in Fig 1.1 when RL = 50 [kΩ].

I = Vs/(R1+R2); I = 5 [V] / ( 10 [kΩ] + 50 [kΩ] ); I = 0.083 [mA]

Conclusion

In this experiment, we became familiar with two electrical measuring devices -- the digital volt meter, and the oscilloscope. The oscilloscope can provide more information when measuring AC signals, but can be cumbersome to connect, due to it's grounding requirements. The digital volt meter can easy to use, but cannot measure peak-to-peak voltage.
